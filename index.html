Bukkoku-ji Zen Monastery

About

Temple Image

Bukkoku-ji (佛國寺), located in Obama City, Fukui Prefecture, is a Zen monastery of the Sōtō Zen tradition. It was long guided by the late Harada Daisetsu Tangen Rōshi, and is now under the leadership of Abbot Harada Kōgaku. The temple follows the Sōtō Zen lineage of meditation and monastic practice.

Zen Village

Daily Life Image

Life at Bukkoku-ji is dedicated to the practice of Zen in daily activities. Monks and lay practitioners share in zazen, chanting, work practice (samu), and communal life, embodying simplicity and mindfulness.

Practice

Zazen Image

Zazen (sitting meditation) is the heart of the practice at Bukkoku-ji. The monastery also hosts sesshin (intensive meditation retreats) throughout the year, open to both Japanese and international practitioners.

Teachings

Dharma Talk Image

Dharma talks and teachings are offered regularly, both at the temple and through recordings. Practitioners are invited to learn directly from the abbot and senior monks.
